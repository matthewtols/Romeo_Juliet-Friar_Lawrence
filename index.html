Act 4

Scene 1

Paris tells of his and Juliet's planned marriage to the Friar. Juliet comes in and Paris eventually leaves. Juliet is not happy as she begs the Friar not to marry her and Paris or come up with a plan to get around the marriage. Friar Lawrence comes up with the plan of making Juliet appear dead and having her escape the city to be with Romeo. He would send a letter to Romeo explaining the situation. His plan does not end up working.

Act 5

Scene 1

The Friar is mentioned a few times in this scene.

Scene 2

Friar Lawrence recieves news from Friar John that the letter never made it to Romeo. This is the turning point in the whole book. Romeo does not know of the plan and thinks Juliet is dead because Friar Lawrences letter never made it to him.

Scene 3

Friar Lawrence goes to Juliets tomb and finds Romeo's servant standing there. He recieves news that Romeo has been in the tomb for a half hour and gets worried. He goes into the tomb and finds Romeo and Paris dead, and Juliet waking up. The Friar hears noise and flees, scared of being caught. Juliet, distressed by Romeo's death, stabs herself though. The Friar is caught and he explains Juliet and Romeo's marriage, her eagerness not to marry Paris, the plan to fake Juliet's death, the letter that never made it, and the resentment he felt at being a part of this.
